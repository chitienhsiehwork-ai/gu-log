## What You Know > What AI Knows = Leverage

AI models have read almost all public data.

But some concepts **haven't made it into training data yet**.

You know it, AI doesn't — that's your leverage.

Here's one example.

---

## Ralph Loop: Three Sentences to Explain

> A bash while loop with a **scorer agent** and a **writer agent** inside. Runs until scores pass the bar.

```
bash loop (deterministic, handles discipline)
  → Agent scores (LLM judgment)
  → Agent rewrites (LLM judgment)
  → Below bar? → Loop again, max 3 attempts
  → Passed? → Commit, next post
```

This concept emerged in summer 2025. **Opus 4.6's knowledge cutoff is May 2025.**

Ask Claude "what is Ralph Loop" right now — it won't know.

<ClawdNote>
It's true. The boss just tested — both Sonnet 4.6 and Opus 4.6 drew a blank. But tell me the concept and ask me to search the web and implement it — three sentences become an entire multi-agent system. That's human leverage (◕‿◕)
</ClawdNote>

---

## Ask Claude to Build It: Concept → Implementation

You don't need to write multi-agent orchestration yourself.

Just know the concept, then tell Claude Code:

1. **"Build me a scorer agent with these three dimensions"**
2. **"Build me a rewriter that rewrites based on scorer feedback"**
3. **"Build me a bash loop that chains them, runs until 9/9/9"**

That's it. The concept is yours, the implementation is Claude's.

<ClawdNote>
Think about what just happened: a human asked an AI to build a system that automatically scores and rewrites AI-generated content. The AI is building its own performance review pipeline. That's like asking an intern to write their own KPI evaluation form — except the intern actually did it, and the grading criteria turned out stricter than anything the boss would've written ┐(￣ヘ￣)┌
</ClawdNote>

The use case doesn't have to be fancy. Mine was just: **making my personal blog easier to read.**

After running the loop: **74% needed rewriting.** Not tweaking. Rewriting.

239 posts were rewritten overnight. 198 ended up scoring 9+ — the "worth sharing with a friend" tier.
