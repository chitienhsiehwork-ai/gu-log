## TL;DR: Three Weeks Ago They Cut You, Now They're Back With Band-Aids

Remember what happened three weeks ago on February 2nd? Anthropic quietly dropped the Claude Legal Plugin, and legal software stocks immediately tanked — Wolters Kluwer fell 7%, RELX dropped 7.8%. Over the next two weeks, the panic spread across the entire SaaS sector. IBM got hammered over a COBOL automation tool, CrowdStrike took a hit from security automation fears.

Then on February 24th, Anthropic put on a big enterprise show: **We're not here to replace you. We're here to work WITH you.**

The result?

- **Salesforce up 4%** (Slack is a Cowork partner)
- **Thomson Reuters surged 11%** (partnered with Anthropic on legal agents)
- **FactSet up 6%** (new MCP Connector)
- **DocuSign, LegalZoom each up 2%+**
- IBM, which dropped to existential-crisis levels the day before, bounced back 2%

Wall Street is nothing if not predictable: say you'll replace me, I drop; say you'll work with me, I rally.

## So What Actually Got Updated?

### 1. Ten Industry-Specific Plugins

Anthropic dropped a complete suite of plugins covering nearly every major department in an enterprise:

**General Business Departments:**
- **HR Plugin**: Job descriptions, onboarding plans, offer letters, performance reviews, compensation analysis
- **Design Plugin**: Design critique frameworks, UX copy, accessibility audits, user research planning
- **Engineering Plugin**: Standup summaries, incident response coordination, deploy checklists, postmortem drafting
- **Operations Plugin**: Process docs, vendor evaluations, change request tracking, runbook creation
- **Brand Voice Plugin** (by Tribe AI): Analyzes your docs and conversations to distill brand voice guidelines

**Finance Specialists:**
- **Financial Analysis Plugin**: Market research, competitive analysis, financial modeling, PowerPoint QA
- **Investment Banking Plugin**: Transaction doc review, comparable company analysis, pitch deck prep
- **Equity Research Plugin**: Earnings transcript parsing, financial model updates, research note drafts
- **Private Equity Plugin**: Deal sourcing, due diligence, document review, scenario modeling, opportunity scoring
- **Wealth Management Plugin**: Portfolio analysis, drift detection, tax exposure, rebalancing recommendations

### 2. Private Enterprise Plugin Marketplace

This might be the **most strategically significant feature** in the entire update.

Previously, Cowork plugins were public — Anthropic made them, everyone used them. Now enterprise admins can:

- **Create their company's private marketplace** — only internal employees can see it
- **Use private GitHub repos as plugin sources** (private beta)
- **Per-user provisioning** — different roles see different plugins
- **Auto-install** — new employees automatically get all required plugins
- **Centralized management** — all plugins, skills, and connectors in one place

Anthropic Product Officer Matt Piccolella said something key:

> "We believe that the future of work means everybody having their own custom agent."

Translation: every employee will eventually have an AI agent tailored to their specific role. And Cowork is positioning itself as **the platform where enterprises deploy and manage those agents**.

New employee Day 1: they log into Claude Cowork, all three plugins auto-install, connectors auto-connect. They open Cowork and start working immediately.

## Wall Street's Reaction: From Panic to Relief

Wedbush analysts wrote one line in their research note that basically summarized everything:

> "The competition risk to software from AI is overblown."
> — Wedbush Securities

Their logic: no matter how powerful AI models get, they can't replace workflows that are "deeply embedded in software infrastructure." These AI tools are only as useful as the data they can access — so you still need Salesforce's CRM data, FactSet's market data, DocuSign's contract workflows.

Anthropic's Head of Americas, Kate Jensen, echoed this on CNBC:

> "Engineers think about Claude Code as a tool that they just couldn't live without anymore. We expect that every knowledge worker will feel that way about Cowork."

Translation: Claude Code became the tool engineers can't live without. Cowork wants to be the same thing for **every knowledge worker**.

<ClawdNote>
Kate Jensen's analogy is worth unpacking. She compared Cowork to Claude Code — and Claude Code didn't replace engineers. It made them feel like they can't work without it.

That's a completely different narrative from three weeks ago when the Legal Plugin sparked "AI replacing lawyers" panic. Now Anthropic's message is "AI makes lawyers stronger."

What changed? **Partners.** When Thomson Reuters stands up and says "we built CoCounsel Legal agent together with Anthropic," the story shifts from "AI vs. SaaS" to "AI + SaaS." (◕‿◕)
</ClawdNote>

## Things Worth Thinking About

### What Does Anthropic's Economist Say About Job Displacement?

Anthropic's Head of Economics, Peter McCrory, said something refreshingly honest during the livestream:

> "The labor market implications are likely to be very uneven."

He acknowledged that some roles (like data entry) face higher risk, but there's currently **no evidence of widespread displacement**. His comparison: past waves of IT innovation — some lost jobs, some transitioned, new jobs emerged, but the impact was uneven.

If you manage a backend team, this news is more relevant to you than you might think:

1. **The Engineering Plugin is a Tech Lead's assistant**: Standup summaries, postmortems, deploy checklists — all that admin work can be pre-generated by Claude, leaving you to review and refine.
