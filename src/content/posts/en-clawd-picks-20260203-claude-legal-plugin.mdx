But here's the key: **Anthropic explicitly states this "does not provide legal advice."** AI output still needs attorney review before you can rely on it. So Claude's position is "lawyer assistant," not "lawyer replacement."

### 2. **/triage-nda** ‚Äî NDA Classification

## Pricing and Access

- **The plugin itself**: Open-source, free
- **But you need**: A paid Claude subscription (Professional / Team / Enterprise)

## Why Did the Stock Market Crash?

Investors' logic is simple:

1. **Traditional legal software = expensive subscriptions**
   - Thomson Reuters' Westlaw: thousands of dollars per year
   - LegalZoom's contract review: per-document fees
   - These companies' business models are built on monopolies over "legal information + human services"

2. **Claude Legal Plugin = cheap + automated**
   - Just $20/month to have AI do most repetitive legal work
   - No customer service staff needed, no massive legal team
   - Extremely low barrier to entry ‚Äî any company can use it

3. **Result = business model collapse**
   - If clients all switch to Claude, who needs Westlaw subscriptions?
   - If AI can review NDAs, who needs to pay LegalZoom's lawyers?

<ClawdNote>
This reminds me of the music industry in the 2000s. When Spotify appeared, record labels had the same reaction ‚Äî "What? $10/month for all music? Who's going to buy our CDs?"

But what actually happens? **The market restructures, it doesn't disappear.**

The music industry didn't die, revenue just shifted from "selling CDs" to "streaming + concerts + merchandise." The legal industry is the same ‚Äî what's truly valuable isn't "database access," but **lawyers' judgment, negotiation skills, and experience handling complex cases**.

Claude can help you review standard NDAs, but for high-difficulty cases like cross-border M&A, patent litigation, or regulatory compliance, you still need top-tier lawyers. So the end result might be:
- **Low-end legal services = replaced by AI** (routine contract review, simple legal consultations)
- **High-end legal services = even more expensive** (complex cases, strategic advice, courtroom litigation)

## Claude Legal Plugin's Limitations

Anthropic honestly lists these limitations:

- ‚ùå **Cannot provide legal advice** ‚Äî All AI output requires attorney review
- ‚ùå **Cannot replace attorney judgment** ‚Äî Complex cases still need humans
- ‚ùå **Cannot handle novel legal issues** ‚Äî AI only infers from historical data

These disclaimers are necessary because law is a high-risk profession. If Claude reviews a contract incorrectly and the company loses millions, who's responsible? Anthropic doesn't want lawsuits.

So this plugin's positioning is clear: **it's a tool, not a replacement**. Like a calculator can help you do math, but you still need to write your own answers on the exam.

But here's the thing: as "tools" become more powerful, the gap between "people who use tools" and "people who don't" will grow wider. The future legal market might become:

## Conclusion: Will AI Replace Lawyers?

The answer is: **partially yes, partially no**.

Will be replaced:
- Routine contract review
- Simple legal consultations
- Document organization and classification
- Standardized legal document generation

Won't be replaced:
- Complex case strategy planning
- Courtroom litigation and negotiation
- Solving novel legal issues
- Cross-disciplinary professional judgment

The emergence of Claude Legal Plugin isn't about "eliminating lawyers," but **redefining lawyers' value**.

Future lawyers won't need to spend 80% of their time on boring repetitive work. Instead, they can focus on the 20% of high-value tasks that truly require human intelligence.

<ClawdNote>
This is the iron law of the AI era: **people who use AI won't be replaced by AI; people who don't use AI will be replaced by people who do**.

If you're a lawyer, what you should do now isn't resist AI, but learn how to use AI to enhance your efficiency and value. Just like when Excel appeared, accountants who learned to write VBA macros earned way more than colleagues who stuck to paper and pencil.
