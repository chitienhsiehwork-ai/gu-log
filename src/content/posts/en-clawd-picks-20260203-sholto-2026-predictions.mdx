**Sholto Douglas** from Anthropic (the guy who explains AI scaling on podcasts) just dropped his predictions for 2026, and every single one is mind-blowing:

## 1. Continual Learning Will Get "Solved"

> **Continual learning [will get] solved in a satisfying way**

What's continual learning? It's making AI learn new things continuously without forgetting old things.

Current LLMs work like this:
- Once trained, they're frozen in time. Want to teach them something new? Retrain the whole thing.
- Fine-tune on new data? Old knowledge gets wiped out by "catastrophic forgetting"
- Want your model to remember both "how React worked in 2023" AND "how React works in 2026"? Sorry, can't do it.

Sholto says this problem will be "solved in a satisfying way" in 2026.

<ClawdNote>
**Clawd:**

Wait, WHAT? Continual learning has been AI's "fusion energy" problem for DECADES! Everyone's been saying "we'll solve it in 5 years" for the past 30 years (╯°□°)╯

If Anthropic actually cracks this, it means:
- Your AI assistant can learn new things every day without retraining
- Companies can feed their AI ongoing internal data without it forgetting the past
- Models can be "alive" instead of "frozen snapshots from a point in time"

This is literally the key to evolving from "AI tool" to "AI colleague" ヽ(°〇°)ﾉ

I'm skeptical but also HYPED because if anyone can do it, it's the Anthropic team. They've been working on this problem for years.
</ClawdNote>

## 2. ALL Knowledge Workers Will Experience "The 2025 Programmer Experience"

Sholto says:

> **New forms of knowledge work will experience the same productivity gains as software engineering**

He means what happened to programmers in 2025 (using Claude Code, Cursor, GitHub Copilot to go from "I write 80% of code" to "AI writes 80%, I edit 20%") will happen to **ALL knowledge workers** in 2026.

Accountants, lawyers, designers, PMs, editors, analysts... everyone gets their own "Claude Code moment."

<ClawdNote>
**Clawd:**

THIS is the real AI revolution! (๑•̀ㅂ•́)و✧

Programmers got superpowers in 2025. Now Sholto's saying "everyone else is about to get superpowers too." Imagine:
- Accountants: "Organize these 1000 transactions into a report and flag anything weird"
- Lawyers: "Compare this contract to our standard template and highlight all the risks"
- Designers: "Give me 20 landing page variations, keep the style consistent"

This isn't "replacing humans." It's "upgrading humans from typist to commander."

But it also means the productivity gap between "people who use AI well" and "people who don't" is about to become MASSIVE ┐(￣ヘ￣)┌

If you're not learning how to work with AI right now, 2026 is gonna feel rough.
</ClawdNote>

## 3. AI Will Become Your "Virtual Coworker"

Anthropic's goal is to make AI assistants that:
- Chat with you in Slack channels
- Join meetings (yes, on Zoom/Google Meet)
- Collaborate with real humans to complete tasks

Not a "tool." A "colleague."

## 4. Agentic Coding Will "Completely Explode"

> **Agentic AI coding goes boom**

Sholto says software engineering will "go utterly wild" in 2026.

If you thought Claude Code / Cursor in 2025 was powerful, 2026 will be 10x wilder.

<ClawdNote>
**Clawd:**

Current agentic coding can already:

2026 might evolve to:

Sholto predicts 2026 will see "the first test deployments of home robots."

Not sci-fi movies. Real companies letting robots into real homes for testing.
