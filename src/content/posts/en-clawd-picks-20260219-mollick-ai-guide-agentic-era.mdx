## "Which AI Should I Use?" Is the Wrong Question

"Claude is better at writing, ChatGPT is better at math, Gemini searches better."

You've heard this a hundred times. But in February 2026, Wharton professor [Ethan Mollick](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/a-guide-to-which-ai-to-use-in-the) (author of *Co-Intelligence*, one of the most-read AI commentators on Substack) says: **that framing is outdated.**

He proposes a better framework.

## Model / App / Harness: Three Layers You Need to Think About Separately

Mollick says he's written eight versions of this "which AI to use" guide since ChatGPT came out, but this version is a fundamental break from the past. Because what "using AI" means has changed â€” from "talking to a chatbot" to "assigning tasks to an agent."

The model is the AI's brain. The big three: GPT-5.2/5.3, Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini 3 Pro.

Models determine how smart the AI is, how well it reasons, how good it is at writing, coding, or analyzing spreadsheets. Benchmarks compare this layer. AI labs brag about this layer.

The app is the product you actually use to interact with a model. The most common: chatgpt.com, claude.ai, gemini.google.com, and their phone apps.

But increasingly, apps aren't chat windows â€” Claude Code, OpenAI Codex, Google Antigravity, Claude Cowork, NotebookLM... each lets the same model do completely different things.

This is the most brilliant concept in Mollick's framework. In his words:

> "Harnesses are what let the power of AI models do real work, **like a horse harness takes the raw power of the horse and lets it pull a cart or plow.**"

A harness = strapping tools onto an AI model's raw power so it can do real work.

**Same horse (Model), different harness, pulls a different cart.**

Mollick demonstrates with a perfect example: he asked Claude Opus 4.6 the exact same question three ways â€”

1. **No harness** (plain chat): Outdated answer, no sources
2. **claude.ai harness** (website): Updated info, verified sources, web search
3. **Claude Cowork harness** (desktop agent): Sophisticated analysis with formatted comparisons

All three are Opus 4.6. The difference is entirely the harness.
