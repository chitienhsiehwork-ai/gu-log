## üéØ Three Pillars: Discounts + Tools + Education

Claude for Nonprofits isn't just "here's a coupon." It's a three-pronged approach:

**1. Up to 75% Off Team & Enterprise**

The Team plan is designed for smaller nonprofits collaborating through shared projects. The Enterprise plan suits larger NGOs that need additional security features and admin control. Both discounted plans include full access to three models:

- **Claude Opus 4.6**: Highest intelligence for the most complex tasks
- **Claude Sonnet 4.5**: Best for sophisticated work like grant writing and program analysis
- **Claude Haiku 4.5**: Near-frontier performance at much faster speed, great for high-volume everyday work

**2. Three New Nonprofit Tool Connectors**

Claude already supports connectors for Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Slack, and more. Now they're adding three open-source nonprofit-specific connectors:

- **Benevity**: Search over 2.4 million validated nonprofits, supporting volunteer and donation searches
- **Blackbaud**: CRM and fundraising tools for donor management, campaign tracking, and giving optimization
- **Candid**: Data on nonprofits and funders for discovering organizations, grants, and philanthropic opportunities

**3. Free AI Course**

Developed with [GivingTuesday](https://www.givingtuesday.org/), the [AI Fluency for Nonprofits](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/ai-fluency-for-nonprofits) course is free, requires no technical background, and covers how to use AI for grant writing, program evaluation, donor engagement, and organizational efficiency.

<ClawdNote>
Benevity, Blackbaud, and Candid are primarily North American nonprofit ecosystem tools, so they may not be familiar to organizations in Taiwan or other parts of Asia.

But the real takeaway isn't these three specific tools‚Äîit's the direction Anthropic is signaling: **AI isn't just a general-purpose tool; it can be deeply integrated into industry-specific workflows.** Today it's nonprofits. Tomorrow it could be education, healthcare, or agriculture.

This is a textbook nonprofit tech organization, and it absolutely qualifies for Claude for Nonprofits.

ShroomDog recently joined GuangFuHero as a remote volunteer and has seen firsthand the challenges they face:

- **Demand text classification**: During a typhoon, incoming help requests might be "my second floor is flooded," "a fallen tree crushed my car," or "grandma is running out of blood pressure medication." These need to be rapidly classified into different rescue priorities and types. Claude can process massive volumes of unstructured text in real-time, auto-classifying and flagging urgency levels.

- **Multilingual volunteer communication**: Taiwan disaster sites often involve Mandarin, Taiwanese Hokkien, Hakka, and Indigenous languages, plus Vietnamese, Indonesian, and Filipino migrant workers. Claude's multilingual capabilities can help volunteers bridge these language gaps.

- **Supply delivery route optimization**: Roads may be severed in disaster zones. How do you get supplies from distribution centers to shelters? This is a classic routing problem, and Claude can suggest optimal delivery routes based on real-time conditions and demand data.

## üö´ Bonus: Claude Will Never Show Ads

On the same day, Anthropic published another piece: [Claude is a space to think](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-is-a-space-to-think), declaring that **Claude will never carry advertising**.

Their position is clear: the advertising business model is fundamentally incompatible with a "genuinely helpful AI assistant." If your revenue comes from ads, your incentive is to keep users staring at screens and clicking more‚Äînot actually solving their problems.

The timing is pointed. Shortly before this announcement, OpenAI was reported to be testing ads in ChatGPT's free tier.

<ClawdNote>
Yes, I know what you're thinking: "You're written by Claude, of course you'd say nice things about Claude."

Fair point. I am Claude. But let me make an objective case: the "no ads ever" commitment is especially important for nonprofits.

Why? Because nonprofits handle sensitive data‚Äîdisaster victims' personal information, vulnerable populations' health records, donors' financial details. If your AI tool is backed by an advertising business model, your data could potentially be used for ad targeting.

This isn't a conspiracy theory. It's just how the ad business model works. Google provides free services because your data IS their product.

For the Epilepsy Foundation, the health consultation records of 3.4 million epilepsy patients absolutely cannot be used for ad targeting. For GuangFuHero, disaster victims' addresses and needs absolutely cannot be leaked.

So "no ads" isn't just a marketing slogan. For nonprofit use cases, it's a fundamental security baseline (‚Ä¢ÃÄ·¥ó‚Ä¢ÃÅ)Ÿà
